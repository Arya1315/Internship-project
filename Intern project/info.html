Follow steps to change battery

Step 1: Arrive at Battery Swap Station

The first step in the battery swapping process is to drive your electric vehicle to the nearest battery swapping station. These stations are equipped with the necessary infrastructure to perform quick battery exchanges. Some stations may require prior booking through a mobile application, especially in busy areas. The driver positions the vehicle at the designated spot for the swap process.

Step 2: Vehicle Authentication

Once the vehicle is positioned correctly, authentication is required to initiate the swapping process. This can be done using an RFID card, scanning a QR code from the mobile app, or through automatic number plate recognition. The system verifies the user's identity, subscription details, or payment options before proceeding.

Step 3: Battery Compartment Access

After successful authentication, the battery compartment is accessed. In most modern EVs, this process is automated, where the station unlocks the battery compartment using its system. In some setups, the driver may need to manually open the battery area to facilitate removal.

Step 4: Remove Depleted Battery

Once the battery compartment is open, the system or station personnel safely remove the depleted battery pack from the vehicle. Automated robotic arms are commonly used for this process to ensure safety and efficiency. Before replacement, the system may run checks on the removed battery to assess its health and usability.

Step 5: Install Fully Charged Battery

A fully charged and tested battery pack is then installed in the vehicle. The system ensures proper alignment and secure connection of the new battery. This step is crucial to guarantee that the vehicle receives a battery in optimal condition, ready for use.

Step 6: System Diagnostics Check

After installing the new battery, the vehicle runs a quick system diagnostic to confirm that the battery is properly connected and functioning as expected. The driver receives confirmation on the vehicle dashboard or through the mobile application, ensuring everything is in order.

Step 7: Payment and Billing

The next step involves processing the payment for the battery swap. Depending on the service model, users may be charged on a subscription basis or through a pay-per-swap system. Payment is often handled automatically via the mobile app, and a digital receipt is generated for the userâ€™s record.

Step 8: Ready to Drive

Once the diagnostics are successful and payment is completed, the driver is free to continue their journey. The entire battery swapping process typically takes around 3 to 5 minutes, providing a convenient and time-efficient alternative to traditional charging methods.
